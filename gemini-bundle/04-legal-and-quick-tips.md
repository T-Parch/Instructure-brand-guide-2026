## Legal Rules

### No possessive trademarks
Do not make company or product names possessive. Trademark law treats trademarked names as adjectives — possessive forms jeopardize trademark status.

- ✗ "Canvas's interface"
- ✓ "The Canvas interface"
- ✗ "Instructure's platform"
- ✓ "The Instructure platform"

### Company proximity requirement
Product names like Canvas and Impact require **company proximity** in marketing contexts:
- The Instructure logo on the same page/ad, OR
- "Instructure" in the copy, OR
- A linked reference on the same page/packaging

### When to flag for legal review
Send to legal before publishing if content includes:
- Factual claims about the organization or its offerings
- Claims about competitors
- Statements about strategic direction
- Numerical data or comparative language

## 10 Quick Tips (Full List)

1. **Sentence case everywhere** — headlines, headers, labels, subject lines, CTAs. Exception: title case for published work titles.

2. **Oxford comma in NORAM** — serial comma before "and" or "or" in lists of three or more.

3. **No ampersands** — designers may occasionally make exceptions.

4. **No possessive trademarks** — Canvas's → the Canvas (see Legal section above).

5. **Favor active voice** — passive voice is acceptable to avoid blame or self-emphasis, but active is the default.

6. **Use contractions** — we're, you'll, it's. Sounds human.

7. **Center the reader, not the software** — avoid making the product the hero.
   **Ban list:** Harness the power of · Unlock · Drive · Leverage · Utilize · Empower · Powerful · Meaningful · Future-proof

8. **Invest in headlines** — attention-grabbing, purpose-driven, with a clear perspective. Don't mail it in.

9. **Don't let AI think for you** — AI is an outline generator, syntax adviser, and style checker. The brand voice comes from you.

10. **When in doubt, check** — brand@instructure.com or #brand-questions.
