## Composition layout pattern (all product brands)

Every composition banner across Canvas, Canvas Career, Mastery, and Parchment follows this consistent structure. Product color appears **only** in non-essential decorative elements.

### Six-part structure
1. **Product logo** — top-left
2. **Large headline** — below the logo on the left half
3. **Body copy** — below the headline (smaller weight, same column)
4. **Photo** — right half, masked into a curved or circular shape that bleeds off the right edge
5. **Decorative solid circles** — in the product's primary brand color, varying sizes, placed at the edges of the image mask or in corners
6. **Negative space** — around the headline column

### Two color variants

**Dark variant (primary)**
| Element | Treatment |
|---------|----------|
| Background | Instructure Navy (`#0b1722`) |
| Logo | Reversed (white) version |
| Headline | Instructure Sky (`#18c2ea`) |
| Body copy | White |
| Decorative circles | Product brand color |

**Light variant**
| Element | Treatment |
|---------|----------|
| Background | White |
| Logo | Full-color version (with product-color icon) |
| Headline | Instructure Navy (`#0b1722`) |
| Body copy | Instructure Navy (`#0b1722`) |
| Decorative circles | Product brand color |

### Decorative circle rules
The solid circles are the **only** place where product brand color appears in compositions. They're accent shapes, not content containers.

- One large circle overlapping the top or bottom edge of the photo mask
- One smaller circle in the opposite corner or floating in negative space near the photo
- Sometimes a third circle of medium size
- Circles **never** overlap text, contain text, or sit behind the logo

## Key brand rules summary

- All core product logos (Canvas, Canvas Career, Mastery, Parchment) appear **only** in their primary horizontal form, in **two colorways**, and **must include the "By Instructure" byline** without exception
- Vertical orientations of core product logos are no longer used
- Product-specific brand colors can't be used in typography or as essential information color due to accessibility contrast requirements
- Navy is used for dark backgrounds; Sky is used for headlines on dark backgrounds
- Co-branding caps assets at two logos max
- The Instructure logo is a bold, modern, geometric, sans-serif word mark with a red dot

## Brand strategy

Instructure follows an **endorsed brand strategy**: the parent brand (Instructure) takes the lead in corporate comms, ecosystem-level storytelling, and company awareness campaigns, but the product sub-brands (Canvas, Mastery, and Parchment) can take starring roles in lower-funnel and product-centric campaigns — always appearing in "by Instructure" lockups.

---

## The Instructure logo

A bold, modern, geometric, sans-serif word mark with a red dot signifying that Instructure is the complete infrastructure for the future of education.

**Variants:**
- **Primary logo** — full word mark, shown over white or dark fields for max contrast
- **Logo + tagline lockup** — pairs the logo with "Setting potential in motion." Use for "mic drop" moments: brand video end cards, presentation closers, select swag
- **Logo mark** — capital "I" + dot. Use when the full logo can't fit

### Dos
- Use on solid, contrasting backgrounds in Instructure brand colors
- Default to the full-color version with the red dot whenever possible
- For single-color or reversed needs, use white-reversed or Instructure Navy

### Don'ts
- Don't place on any color other than black, white, Instructure Navy, or one of the tertiary blues
- Don't type out your own logo
- Don't change the dot's color
- Don't place over photos or color-overlaid photos

## Co-branding guidelines (NEW — May 2026)

To balance corporate equity with established product recognition, follow these rules:

### Hierarchy rules
- The **Instructure logo is our North Star**. Where possible, it occupies the primary brand position (top-left or prominent footer)
- Avoid logo soup: max two logos per asset
- Let campaign type and asset type drive your logo strategy

### Guidance by campaign and asset type

**Corporate awareness campaigns and assets**
Use the Instructure color palette and make the Instructure logo the sole hero.

**Product-specific campaigns and assets**
Lead with the product logo and color palette. Make sure the Instructure logo is present in an anchoring position to ground the product in the ecosystem and provide cross-sell visibility.

**Small-scale assets (social, display ads, swag)**
On space-constrained assets, prioritize the Instructure logo. When a product logo is advisable (e.g., top-of-funnel product awareness), prioritize the [Product] by Instructure logo lockup.

**Event booths**
Use the Instructure logo for high-level signage to define the space, and product logos for specific zones, demo stations, or workstations within the booth. Don't use more than two logos on a single banner or backdrop.

## Illustration

### Approach
Custom illustrations preferred over stock photography. Establishes unique brand identity.

**Inspiration:** Abbey Lossing, Andy J. Pizza

### Principles
1. **Style:** Flat, 2D. No gradients, no intricate shading.
2. **Color:** Thoughtful, balanced application. Mirrors brand palette hierarchy.
3. **Composition:** Distinct focal points. Simple, clear narratives. Subtle dot usage encouraged.
4. **Characters:** Diverse, relatable, natural emotions aligned with brand values.
5. **Avoid:** Stock visual clichés and overused tropes.
6. **Commissioning:** Provide full brief with guidelines, palettes, and style references.

## We believe in accessibility for all

Just like learners, our customers (present and future) have a wide range of needs and abilities. We do everything we can to ensure everyone can experience and interact with our site, content, and products in the ways that work best for them.

The focus for design is **color, contrast, and legibility**, plus structure, alt text, links, and captions for content.

**Resources:** WebAIM is the go-to source for accessibility best practices. For Instructure-specific questions, use @marketing-a11y or #brand-questions.

---

## Color

The Instructure palette is a horizon of blues (a good color for accessibility) with deep Instructure Navy as the foundation.

## Contrast

Contrast is non-negotiable. Use the WebAIM Contrast Checker to verify ratios using hex codes.

**WCAG 2.0 AA** requires a contrast ratio of at least **4.5:1 for normal text**. The goal is for all copy and essential information (including logos and CTAs) to meet these ratios.

Sample: Instructure Sky (`#18c2ea`) on Instructure Navy (`#0b1722`) = 8.57:1.

### Color and contrast dos
- Text on solid color backgrounds, in approved Instructure tones
- WCAG 2.2: standard text **4.5:1** minimum
- Functional UI elements (button borders, icons, form fields) **3:1** minimum against background

### Color and contrast don'ts
- Don't use Mastery, Canvas, or Parchment product colors for headline text or other essential elements
- Avoid red+green combos (color blindness)
- Don't place typography or logos over photographs or illustrations
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning ("click the red button" — use icons or text labels too)

---

## Legibility and type scale

Following a simple type scale helps with accessibility, hierarchy, and clarity.

Sample: headline 48pt, body copy 24pt.

**Minimum digital type size: 12pt** (per the accessibility page). Set base at 12pt and move in 4pt increments (12, 16, 20, 24, 28…).

> ⚠️ Note: The typography page lists 16pt as the minimum. The two pages refer to different contexts — confirm with brand which applies for your asset (UI vs. body copy).

---

## Structure and headings (NEW — May 2026)

A digital document needs a logical flow. Proper heading structure lets screen readers navigate efficiently and understand section relationships.

- **Logical hierarchy.** Use only one `<h1>` per page (the main title). Follow with `<h2>` for sections, `<h3>` for subsections.
- **Don't skip levels.** Never jump from `<h2>` to `<h4>`. Consistency clarifies the information.
- **Tables.** Use tables for data only, never for layout.

---

## Slide and email design (NEW — May 2026)

Presentations and emails should provide a cohesive brand experience while staying accessible.

- **The template is key.** Use the Instructure core slide template. Don't modify it — especially not the prescribed colors or type sizes set by marketing and learning strategy.
- **Email simplicity.** Keep email layouts simple. Avoid complex tables for layout. Use 12pt as your base for body copy.

---

## Alternative text (NEW — May 2026)

Alt text provides a text-based bridge for visual elements.

- **Be descriptive.** Concise, meaningful descriptions for images that convey information (e.g., "Student using a laptop with a teacher looking on")
- **Decorative images.** If purely aesthetic, mark as decorative so screen readers can skip
- **Complex graphics.** For charts or diagrams, brief summary in alt text, full description in surrounding body copy

---

## Meaningful links (NEW — May 2026)

Links should be a clear path, not a guessing game. Raw URLs are difficult for screen readers.

- **No raw URLs.** Avoid long strings like "https://sites.google.com/…"
- **Descriptive text.** Use "Learn more about the state of learning" instead of "Click here"

---

## Captions and transcripts (NEW — May 2026)

All audio and video content must be accessible.

- **Captions.** All video content requires synchronized captions for D/deaf or hard of hearing viewers
- **Transcripts.** Provide text-based transcripts for audio and video as an alternative
- **Audio descriptions.** For videos with significant visual information or no dialogue, include a detailed audio description track or text-based narrative

---

## When AI flags accessibility risk

When reviewing or generating content, flag these for fixes:
- Color used as the only signal for meaning
- Contrast ratios below 4.5:1 (text) or 3:1 (UI elements)
- Skipped heading levels
- Raw URLs in body copy
- Missing alt text mentions for image-heavy content
- Video without captions/transcripts
- Tables used for layout instead of data
